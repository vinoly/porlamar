01 — Foundation

A club built on
shared horizons.

Por La Mar — "by the sea" in old Castilian — is a luxury car club rooted in the landscape and spirit of Santa Barbara. It is a place where exceptional automobiles meet genuine community. Not a velvet rope; a shoreline.

The brand draws from the architecture of the California Riviera: white stucco walls, exposed timber beams, terracotta tile, and salt-bleached wood. Materials that hold history without announcing it.

Brand Pillars

Community

Rooted in Santa Barbara. Open to all who love the drive.

Grounded Luxury

Refined taste without pretension. The good life, lived simply.

Place

Santa Barbara is not a backdrop. It is the point.

The Drive

Machines are meant to be used. Routes, rallies, and real roads.

02 — The Mark

Circle & Word.

The mark is drawn from the medallion seals of old Spanish colonial charters — a circle containing place and purpose. The hand-drawn geometry of the letterforms suggests craft and care; neither digital precision nor casual irregularity.

The three-line stacking of POR · LA · MAR creates a visual tension: the word arcs of POR and MAR echo the circle, while LA anchors the center — a horizon line between sky and sea.

Mark Anatomy

Primary · Cream
Secondary · Parchment
Reversed · Ink
Accent · Sea

✓ Do

  • Use the mark at legible sizes (80px+ digital, 1in+ print)
  • Apply only on approved background colors
  • Maintain clear zone equal to the mark's radius on all sides
  • Scale proportionally and uniformly
  • Use reversed (cream) version on all dark and mid-tone backgrounds

✗ Do not

  • Stretch, skew, or distort the mark
  • Apply the mark over busy photography without a containment field
  • Recreate or modify the letterforms
  • Use outline-only versions of the mark
  • Add drop shadows, glows, or visual effects
  • Place the ink version on dark backgrounds

03 — Color

Earth, road, and stucco.

The palette is drawn entirely from the physical world of Santa Barbara: bleached adobe walls, aged teak beams, fired clay roof tile, and the deep institutional green of the California Route 1 highway shield. Warm naturals anchor the system; the material accents give it life.

05 — Voice & Tone

Unhurried.
Honest. Present.

Por La Mar does not shout. It does not perform exclusivity. It speaks like someone who knows the back roads: directly, warmly, without pretension. The tone is always grounded — luxury is implied through restraint, never claimed through excess.

Grounded

We don't talk about cars the way a magazine does. We talk about drives. About the hour before sunrise on East Valley Road, the kind of morning that justifies owning something with a flat-six.

Earned, not claimed

Inclusive

Whether you drive a vintage 911 or a daily Miata, you belong on the same road. Por La Mar is not curated by price point. It is curated by curiosity and care.

Open to all enthusiasts

Specific

We name the roads. We mention the café. The olive grove you pass at mile seven. Good writing about place is more luxury than any brand claim.

Named, concrete, vivid

Spare

White space is not emptiness. It is confidence. We do not fill every silence with copy. We say what needs saying and let the image — or the reader's imagination — do the rest.

Less is always more

Write like this

"Sunday morning. Pacific Coast Highway. Eight cars, no agenda."
"Membership is open to anyone who takes the road seriously."
"Join us this month on the Santa Ynez loop — 90 miles of the best asphalt in the county."
"Reserved for members. Earned through showing up."

Not like this

"Curated driving experiences for the discerning collector."
"Join an elite community of automotive aficionados."
"Experience the pinnacle of luxury motoring lifestyle."
"Por La Mar: Where passion meets performance. ®"
"

They're about the hour, the road, the people you find at the end of it.

Digital Presence

The website and app carry the same restraint as print. Full-bleed photography with minimal typographic overlays. No hover animations that distract from the content. Navigation in Jost, all uppercase and spaced wide.

Sea and terracotta are used exclusively for interactive elements and calls to action — never decoratively.

07 — Photography Direction

Light before 9am
and after 5pm.

Photography should feel discovered, not staged. Early morning or late afternoon light only — the "magic hours" that flatten shadows and warm surfaces. Cars are incidental to landscape; the road is always more important than the machine on it.

The road as subject — landscape over machine
Golden light, every time
Cars as details, not centerpieces
Community over collection
Place before everything
Treatment All images: slight sepia shift (+15%), reduced saturation (−20%), lifted shadows. Analog warmth without Instagram filter.

Always

  • — Magic hour light only
  • — Landscape-dominant framing
  • — Film grain or analog processing
  • — Natural materials in environment
  • — Candid, unposed moments
  • — Desaturated, warm-shifted color grade

Never

  • — Studio lighting
  • — Cars on white backgrounds
  • — Motion blur on wheels
  • — HDR or oversaturated edits
  • — Track or garage settings alone
  • — Posed "hero shots"

Reference

  • — Slim Aarons (people + place)
  • — Pete Eckert (abstraction)
  • — Road & Track circa 1970
  • — Condé Nast Traveler editorial
  • — Wim Wenders still frames
